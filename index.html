Strategic Planning - This Is the Focus Area
Q: Where do you want this operation to be in 6 months if everything goes right?
Honestly, I want to stop being the bottleneck. Right now if I'm out for a week, half the reporting stops with me, and that's not what good ops management looks like. So Phase 2 and 3 matter more to me than they probably sound on a roadmap slide, because that's what gets us off manual pulls. The other thing is the growth programme actually working, not just running. If Harneet and Gaurav are auditors by December and there's a queue of people asking to be next, that's when I'll believe retention is behind us, not just quiet for a quarter.
Q: If you had to cut one of your three roadmap phases to save time, which would you cut?
I wouldn't cut one outright, I'd just be honest about the order. WFM forecasting in Phase 3 is nice to have. QA structure and system reporting in Phase 2 is not, it's basically the floor everything else stands on. If someone made me drop something tomorrow, that's the one I'd protect, because without trustworthy numbers this whole review is just me telling you stories.
Q: How do you decide what to escalate versus solve yourself?
I've got a pretty blunt rule for myself. Floor stuff, rota, breaks, coaching, whether someone's pulling their weight, that's mine, full stop. The second something needs money or a system I don't have the keys to, it goes up, and it goes up with evidence, not a hunch. The salary appraisal and the supplier portal access are both exactly that, I could see the problem clearly, but I don't have the lever, so I brought evidence instead of a guess.
Q: What would you do differently if you were starting this role again from day one?
Push the access requests in week one, not week three. I burned real hours on workarounds, manual tracking, paste-in dashboards, that whole exercise, some of which should've just gone into chasing the portal access sooner instead. Lesson learned: ask first, diagnose while you wait, don't wait for the full picture before you raise the blocker.
Q: If CO or TO grows another 20%, does your current structure hold?
Mostly. The TL setup and the rota don't really care how many people are on the floor, they scale with coverage, not headcount. Where it gets harder is quality. There's no QA Auditor role anymore, that got replaced by an AI tool, Cortex, that scores soft skills on every call. It's new, the transcription still gets things wrong sometimes, and it only covers half the job, nobody's auditing process quality or the case side yet. At 20% more volume that gap gets bigger, not smaller. So the ask now isn't hire an auditor, it's make sure someone's validating what the AI gets wrong while it learns.
Q: What's the single biggest strategic risk to this operation right now?
Retention, and it's not close. Six people told me, directly, that pay is why they're job hunting. That's not me reading tea leaves, that's what they said to my face. The growth programme buys goodwill short term, but if the appraisal doesn't move, I think we lose people we genuinely can't afford to lose, probably the same ones who made this quarter look better than it otherwise would.
